About

I ended up in data through research, which means I learned early that a number without context is just noise. Most of my work has been in public-sector and academic environments where the data is messy, the stakes are real, and the audience is a policymaker, not an analyst. I think in pipelines: source → ingest → transform → serve → visualize. I like the full stack of it: building the schema, running the analysis, and figuring out what it actually means.

Curious about most things — cinema, photography, languages, cooking, humanities. Food and film are usually how I find my way into something new. The rabbit holes are deep and I don’t always find my way back quickly. I think that’s fine.
